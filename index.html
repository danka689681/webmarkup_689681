Santorini

Santorini, being the one of the most iconic Greek Islands, offers mind blowing picturesque views from the white clay buildings built on the side of a hill overlooking the crystal clear blue water. While it is also among the more touristy Greek islands (drinks and food prices suffer because of this), there is no better getaway than soaking in the sunset surrounded by beautiful architecture at the beach. There are also plenty of clubs and jet ski rentals for the ultimate instagram experience.

Crete

Home to the first advanced civilization in Europe, crete has well-preserved ruins from 4000 years ago. The largest of the Greek Islands, it offers great hiking between valleys, over small mountains, and sun soaked beaches. Crete's rich history obviously lends inself to plentiful interesting museums, and its location shares the same weather and similar cuisine to the other Greek Islands. Untouched by mass tourism, Crete still has its culture well preserved, especially villages hidden in mountains in hills. Come emerse yourself in this beautiful island!

Tenerife

Tenerife, located off the coast of Africa and a part of the Canary Islands, is by far one of the most popular islands to visit in the world, with 5 million tourists per year. Most tourists hang out in the southern beaches where there are beutiful views, clubs, and resorts, however, if you want to hike, the island is vast with sub tropical forests and a snow covered volcano to explore. Tenerife also has its own carnival, which comes in second to Rio De Janiero's.

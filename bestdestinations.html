Santorini

Santorini, being the one of the most iconic Greek Islands, offers mind blowing picturesque views from the white clay buildings built on the side of a hill overlooking the crystal clear blue water. While it is also among the more touristy Greek islands (drinks and food prices suffer because of this), there is no better getaway than soaking in the sunset surrounded by beautiful architecture at the beach. There are also plenty of clubs and jet ski rentals for the ultimate instagram experience. Santorini roughly costs 1,000 euros per week to visit, assuming a comfortable stay, however it can be done cheaper by staying in hostiles and mostly eating supermarket food, it can cost half as much per week.

Crete

Home to the first advanced civilization in Europe, crete has well-preserved ruins from 4000 years ago. The largest of the Greek Islands, it offers great hiking between valleys, over small mountains, and sun soaked beaches. Crete's rich history obviously lends inself to plentiful interesting museums, and its location shares the same weather and similar cuisine to the other Greek Islands. Untouched by mass tourism, Crete still has its culture well preserved, especially villages hidden in mountains in hills. Come emerse yourself in this beautiful island! Crete costs also wildly depend on the type of vacation you are looking for (and where you're flying from), but averages around 600 euros a week.

Tenerife

Tenerife, located off the coast of Africa and a part of the Canary Islands, is by far one of the most popular islands to visit in the world, with 5 million tourists per year. Most tourists hang out in the southern beaches where there are beutiful views, clubs, and resorts, however, if you want to hike, the island is vast with sub tropical forests and a snow covered volcano to explore. Tenerife also has its own carnival, which comes in second to Rio De Janiero's. Tenerife costs around 850 euros a week, assuming you're staying in 2 to 3 star hotels.

Cairo

Cairo, containing one of the 7 wonders of the world, is a great climate escape during the winter seasons in western countries. It is relatively cheap to visit, with an average cost of 500 euros for a week, and even cheaper from hostiles, although the quality of some can be quite sketchy. With visiting the great pyramids, indulging in the local agriculture, and riding on the backs of camels, you are guaranteed one of the most iconic African/middle eastern experiences.

Rio de Janeiro

Rio De Janeiro is the second largest city in Brazil. Known for its carnival, the Cristo Redentor statue, breathtaking landscape, excellent football skills, and laidback beach culture, you would be surprised that this city isn't among the most expensive to visit either. With an average cost of 451 euros per week for one person, this city will not break the bank.

Tokyo

When you're in the biggest and most densely populated city in the planet, it's quite difficult not to find things to do. Exploring the vast cultural differences compared to any western society can have people feeling like they're in a different world, along with the delicious cuisine such as sushi. Hostiles cost around 70 euros a day, with a week's stay averaging around 1,000 euros. Flights from Europe can be from 400 to 800 euros.
